              Quick rules

              Each faction is given a list of secret keywords, numbered 1 through 4. Factions cannot see each other's keywords.

              An encryptor from each faction must encrypt a secret message for their comrades. A message consists of three unique digits from 1 to 4, like 413. Encryption is performed by writing three clues, each connecting a digit to a keyword.

              The encryptor's comrades must decrypt the message by interpreting these clues.
              Getting it wrong counts as a ⬤ miscommunication.

              You will also see the enemy's encrypted message, and must try to decrypt it by looking at previous messages and inferring their keywords.
              Getting it right counts as an ⬤ interception.

              A faction wins by ⬤ intercepting two enemy messages, or loses by ⬤ miscommunicating twice.
              If both sides win and lose in the same round,
              there's a tiebreak.
